The reality

Small businesses are targets — not too small to bother with.

It's a common myth that attackers only chase big companies. In practice, small businesses are attractive precisely because they hold valuable data — customer records, payment details, logins — while often running with fewer defences than a large enterprise. Most attacks aren't sophisticated, targeted operations either; they're automated and opportunistic, sweeping the internet for easy openings.

The good news: the same basics stop the vast majority of them. You don't need an enterprise budget — you need to close the obvious gaps consistently. That's what this checklist is for. Get through the essentials first, then use a professional penetration testing service to confirm nothing important slipped through.

Beyond the basics

Ticked the boxes? Now find out if it holds up.

Checklists close the obvious gaps, but they can't tell you whether a determined attacker could still get in. That takes hands-on testing — someone probing your website and systems the way a real attacker would, then reporting exactly what they found and how to fix it.

When it's worth getting a professional test

Before launching a new website or app, after big infrastructure changes, when a client or regulation requires it, or simply to get an honest picture of your exposure. A scoped penetration test turns "we think we're secure" into "we've checked, and here's the proof."
